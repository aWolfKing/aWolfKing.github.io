Dimension
    A game mechanic where the character can switch between multiple dimensions.
    Two dimensions in one view.
    The character can 'peek' into another dimension by holding up her left hand.
    When the character is not at a position where it can switch dimensions, the character will peek into it's current dimension.
    If the character can peek into a different dimension than it currently is in, the character can switch to the dimension it is able to see by 'peeking'.
    Dimension switching in action.
    How it works
    Here I will explain how this dimension switch mechanic works.
    Two of everything

    In order for this mechanic to work there are (at least) 2 camera's, 2 or more 'worlds' and two identical characters.
    2 characters, 2 worlds and 2 camera's.
    Two characters

    To make sure the character you see in the 'peeked' dimension has the lighting and effects of the corresponding dimension we need to make sure there is a second (identical) character rendered in that dimension.
    Of course the different dimension may influence your character and have the character look a different way (or be a different species).
    If this is the case the second character you render could be replaced by a different model.
    Syncing animations

    To make sure both rendered characters are in the exact same pose and animation, we synchronize the bones of both characters.
    All bone rotations are copied to the clone character to have the clone character always be posed the same way the main character is.
    Two worlds

    I wanted to have my character switch between two totally different worlds, so I actually needed to render two worlds, but depending on what you want to achieve you might not need to.
    When you do create two worlds, make sure your character cannot end up inside objects or the terrain while switching.
    I limited the dimension switching to areas of which I was sure there would be no problems when switching dimensions.
    Two cameras

    If there are two dimensions/worlds, there are things that are not meant to be seen in all dimensions.
    Which means those objects or effects need to be rendered only to the camera of the dimension(s) they are in.
    If you have a camera for each existing world or dimension, make sure only the cameras of the current and target dimension are actually active.
    You don't want to render things you won't use.
    Only the camera of the current dimension will be rendered to the screen.
    The camera of the target dimension will only be rendered to a texture, which will be used by the particle effect.
    Two of everything, right?

    Well, I actually used a third camera, not just two.
    This third camera is the only camera that can see the particle effect (and nothing else), this camera will be rendered last and put on top of the other camera's.
    The particle camera rendered on top of a solid background.
    The particle effect

    All particles that are emitted by the particle effect are 3D cubes with a shader effect applied.
    The texture that was rendered by the target dimension's camera is rendered to all 'pixels' of all particles to create the 'dimension peek' effect.
    Screen-uv coördinates are used for this.
    A little uv offset will be added to the screen-uv coördinates to create a distortion effect.
    This offset is based on the particle's normal directions.
    The switch

    The switch works by swapping the position of the character with the character clone and at the same time changing the target dimension's camera to be the current dimension's camera.
    At the point of the switch, the screen is filled with particles and only the target dimension's (now switched to be the current dimension's) camera's rendered texture will be visible.
    Until the particle effect is no longer visible, no camera's will be rendered to that texture in order to mask the camera swap.
    Otherwise the camera and dimension switch will be clearly notable.
    Because the particle camera is rendered on top of the other camera's the camera switch that takes place is not visible.
