Camouflage
    A game where two players challenge each other and try to find the near invisible box before the other does.
    I started making this game after finding out you could get the color of a specific pixel in Processing.
    Because I found out about this, I decided to make a game that utilizes some kind of camouflage/cloak effect.
    Setup
    You will need to download in order to play the game.
    If you have installed you can the game's code and load it into a Python sketch.
    You can now play the game.
    If you do not want to download the game's code you can also the code in your browser.
    How to play
    Find the near invisible box before the other player does.
    The near invisible box starts at the left side of the screen and moves to the right side.
    The players have to spot the box before it leaves the right side of the screen.
    If they don't the background changes and the box starts at the left side again.
    Colors are generated each time the box spawns at the left side of the screen.
    If one of the players think they spotted the box they need to press their respective 'claim' key.
    For one player this key is 'w' and for the other this key is 'up'.
    Once any claim key is pressed the game notifies which player may click the box to score a point.
    Do you see the box?
    If the player who 'claimed' clicks on the box, the box becomes white to show it's position and speeds up to reach the right side of the screen faster.
    The player who 'claimed' also gets a point.
    The white box is at around the same position the near invisible box was in the previous image.
    Both players can cancel their 'claim' by pressing 's' or 'down' respectively.
    If the player who pressed their 'claim' key clicks at the wrong position or changed their mind and cancels their 'claim', they cannot press their 'claim' key again until the box reached the right or the other player scored a point.
